<p><p>The automotive crankshaft market comprises three main types: **Forged Steel**, **Cast Iron/Steel**, and **Machined Billet**. Forged steel crankshafts are durable, high-strength, and ideal for high-performance engines due to their resistance to stress. Cast iron/steel crankshafts are cost-effective, widely used in standard vehicles, and offer good wear resistance. Machined billet crankshafts are precision-engineered from solid steel blocks, providing exceptional strength and customization for racing or specialized applications. Each type caters to specific performance, cost, and manufacturing requirements in the automotive industry.</p></p>
