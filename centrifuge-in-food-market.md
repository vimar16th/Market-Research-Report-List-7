Vertical centrifuges are compact, space-efficient, and ideal for smaller-scale operations, offering high-speed separation with a smaller footprint. Horizontal centrifuges, on the other hand, are larger, designed for continuous processing, and suited for high-capacity applications like dairy or starch production. Both types enhance efficiency, but vertical models prioritize space-saving, while horizontal models focus on scalability and handling larger volumes in industrial food processing.</p></p>
