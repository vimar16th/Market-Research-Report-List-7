<p><p>The GPS tracking devices market includes three main types: standalone trackers, OBD devices, and advanced trackers. Standalone trackers are portable units used for personal tracking or asset monitoring. OBD (On-Board Diagnostics) devices connect directly to a vehicle's diagnostic port, providing real-time tracking and vehicle diagnostics. Advanced trackers incorporate additional features like geofencing, driver behavior analysis, and enhanced connectivity, making them ideal for fleet management. Each type caters to different user needs, from personal safety to commercial vehicle oversight.</p></p>
