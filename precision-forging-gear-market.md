<p><ul><li>Cold Forging Forming</li><li>Warm Forging Forming</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Precision Forging Gear market encompasses two primary types: cold forging and warm forging. Cold forging, conducted at room temperature, enhances dimensional accuracy and surface finish, making it suitable for high-strength components with intricate designs. In contrast, warm forging involves heating the material to a specific temperature, allowing for improved ductility and decreased forming forces, which is ideal for larger and more complex shapes. Both processes optimize material utilization and mechanical properties, catering to diverse industrial applications.</p></p>
