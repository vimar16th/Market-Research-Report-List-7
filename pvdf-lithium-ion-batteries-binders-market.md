<p><p>In the PVDF lithium-ion battery binders market, two main types of polymerization processes are utilized: emulsion polymerization and suspension polymerization. Emulsion polymerization involves dispersing the monomer in water with surfactants, yielding fine polymer particles suitable for optimal binder properties. In contrast, suspension polymerization involves creating larger polymer beads by suspending monomers in a continuous phase, resulting in materials with distinct physical properties and processing advantages. Both methods play crucial roles in enhancing battery performance by improving adhesion and electrochemical stability.</p></p>
