<p><p>The solder balls market is primarily categorized into two types: lead solder balls and lead-free solder balls. Lead solder balls, composed of alloys containing lead, are traditionally used for their excellent electrical conductivity and reliability in various electronic applications. However, due to environmental and health concerns, lead-free solder balls have gained popularity, featuring alternative alloys like tin, silver, and copper. These lead-free options meet regulatory standards while providing good soldering performance, making them increasingly favored in modern electronics manufacturing.</p></p>
