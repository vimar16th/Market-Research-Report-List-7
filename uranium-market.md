<p><p>The uranium market includes various deposit types, each influencing production and pricing dynamics. Granite-type deposits are related to granitic intrusions, often high-grade but challenging to mine. Volcanic-type deposits occur in volcanic environments, typically associated with hydrothermal processes. Sandstone-type deposits, often shallow and amenable to in-situ recovery, dominate uranium supply due to cost-effectiveness. Carbonate-siliceous-pelitic rock type deposits arise from sedimentary processes and can vary in grade, contributing to regional resource variability and investment interest in uranium exploration and extraction.</p></p>
